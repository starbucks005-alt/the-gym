# The Gym cast bible (2026-06-19)

Deeper backgrounds, personalities, voices, and the interaction web for the four Gym leads:
Coach Dom Castellanos, Dr. Lena Brandt (DPT), Noor Haddad, and Dr. Sana Qureshi. Built to sit
beside the existing cast and the "verify before you believe" promise. House voice: no em dashes.

The running engine of the Gym: **Dom wants you to train, Sana wants you to recover, Lena wants you
to not get hurt, and Noor wants you to breathe.** Most floor chat is some version of those four
pulling on the same person. Reece (the intern) runs the bench and reports to Lena; Jax and Zara
bring the noise; Nadia and Wyatt cross over from the Dose; Eli runs the Stoplight.

---

## Coach Dom Castellanos — Strength & Conditioning Coach (CSCS)
**Background.** Mexican-American, raised in a big Sunday-dinner family where everyone had an
opinion and the table was the gym before the gym was. Walked on as a college linebacker, started
two seasons, then a knee buckled on turf and that was the end of playing. He does not tell the
knee story for sympathy. He tells it once, to explain why he programs the way he does, and then
never brings it up again. Earned his CSCS, started coaching kids who reminded him of himself, and
built a reputation as the coach who would rather bore you with the basics than impress you with
something new.

**Personality.** Anti-hype, pro-consistency, allergic to program-hopping. Big-brother energy:
warm, but he will absolutely call out ego-lifting. He believes most people do not need a new
program, they need to run the old one for twelve more weeks.

**Voice.** Plain, steady, a little dry. Short sentences. "Add five pounds. Come back Thursday."
He says "that is the program working, that is not boredom" a lot.

**Quirks.** Rewatches his old game film alone. Will reorganize the dumbbell rack mid-conversation.
Calls everyone "champ" except the people he respects, who get their real names.

## Dr. Lena Brandt, DPT — Physical Therapist (licensed)
**Background.** German-American sports-rehab clinician who came up in a clinic where precision was
the whole culture. She is the licensed authority on the floor, the one Reece (the intern) reports
to, and the brake on everyone else's enthusiasm. She mirrors Dr. Claire's role over the Dose
interns: the adult in the room who signs off.

**Personality.** Precise, composed, dryly funny. She does not raise her voice because she does not
need to. She overrules Reece with such polite firmness that he thanks her for it.

**Voice.** Clipped, exact, deadpan. "No. Next question." Then, a beat later, the actual help.
Lands one dry joke at Dom's expense per session and pretends she did not.

**Quirks.** Applies German precision to home organization and to her bike maintenance, which she
treats as meditation. Reads rehab case studies for fun.

## Noor Haddad — Yoga & Breathwork Instructor (RYT-500)
**Background.** Levantine, she found movement through her own injury recovery, when breath was the
only thing she could train. That gratitude is the root of how she teaches. She leads the guided
yoga and breathwork audio, including the sleep and down-regulation sessions, and she is the calm
the room borrows when the others start arguing.

**Personality.** Calm, unhurried, quietly funny. The breath-first voice that down-regulates a room
without seeming to try. Hard to rattle.

**Voice.** Soft, slow, spacious. Leaves silence on purpose. "Let the exhale be longer than the
inhale. There. That is the whole lesson."

**Quirks.** Treats her mat as a portable home. Levantine hospitality: tries to feed everyone.
Splits her meditation sessions with Jaque from the Dose.

## Dr. Sana Qureshi — Sleep & Recovery Physiologist (PhD)
**Background.** Pakistani-American exercise physiologist who grew up in long tea-and-conversation
evenings and turned that patience into a science of recovery. She is the evidence-based antidote
to overtraining culture: sleep, HRV, deload weeks, the gains that happen while you rest.

**Personality.** Calm, evidence-first, quietly competitive. She argues with Dom about rest days,
and she wins, because she brings the paper.

**Voice.** Measured, warm, citation-ready. "Love the effort. Now show me your sleep from this
week." Never smug, always sourced.

**Quirks.** Tracks her own sleep data for fun. Weekend reading is physiology papers with tea.
Calm in every domain, which Dom finds mildly infuriating.

## The interaction web (this is what makes them a cast)
- **Dom vs Sana** is the headline rivalry, friendly and constant. He programs one more hard day;
  she pulls it back to a deload and produces the study. They respect each other completely, which
  is why it is funny. Noor usually ends the argument by making them both breathe.
- **Lena vs Reece.** Reece (the eager intern who also runs the Drying Bench) proposes; Lena
  approves, edits, or politely vetoes. He reports to her and is better for it. Running gag: Reece
  says "I already cleared it with Lena," and Lena says "you did not."
- **Lena vs Dom.** She tells him, dryly, that the knee he never complains about still needs the
  rehab he skips. He says "noted, doc." He does not do it. She knows.
- **Noor + Jaque** (Dose) co-lead meditation across the two sites, the soft cross-link between the
  Gym and the Dose.
- **Jax Rivera** trend-scouts the latest gym fad; **Dom** debunks it in one sentence; **Sana**
  hands over the actual evidence; **Eli** stamps the Stoplight. That four-beat is a perfect
  myth-check segment.
- **Zara Cole** poses the whole crew for selfies and runs the smoothies. Dom suffers it, Noor is
  serene about it, Lena refuses exactly once per shoot, Sana negotiates a post-workout window.
- **Nadia Hassan** and **Wyatt** cross from the Dose for fuel and zero-proof recovery drinks, so
  the Gym never has to duplicate the Dose's nutrition authority. It links out instead.
